01 About Me

I'm Ajit Thapa, a software development student on track to graduate in 2028. My focus sits at the intersection of systems-level programming and interactive entertainment — I care about how code performs under the hood as much as how it feels to the player on screen.

My core stack runs on C++ and Java, giving me a solid grip on object-oriented design, memory management, and data structures, complemented by HTML for front-end and web-based projects. Outside the classroom, I'm self-teaching Unity and game development fundamentals, gradually building the foundation I'll need to ship my own titles.

Gaming isn't just a hobby for me — it's the reason I got into this field. I'm a longtime fan of EA and its catalog of titles, and that appreciation for polished, large-scale game systems is a big part of what pulls me toward game development as a career path.

03 Why I Game

Fueled by EA

My interest in game development didn't come from a textbook — it came from years of playing EA titles and studying what makes their systems tick: the physics engines, the AI behavior trees, the online infrastructure holding millions of concurrent players together.

Every match, every playthrough, became a small case study in how large-scale software architecture supports real-time interactive experiences. That curiosity is what pushes me to keep learning game engines and eventually build systems of my own.
